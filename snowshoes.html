Thoreau's Snowshoes
Thoreau walked for hours
every day, all year long.
As he went, he recorded his
observations of the natural world:
the depth of snow,
presence of animal tracks,
and when flowers bloomed
or ice melted.
He recognized the extraordinary
in ordinary events.
